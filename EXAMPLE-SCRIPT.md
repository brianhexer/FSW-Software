INT. NEW YORK COFFEE SHOP - MORNING

The early morning sun streams through large windows. The shop is quiet, only a few early risers scattered about.

SARAH (32, confident but tired) sits at a corner table with a lukewarm cup of coffee. She checks her phone repeatedly.

The bell above the door chimes. JAMES (34, handsome, uncertain) enters. He spots Sarah and hesitates before walking over.

JAMES
Hi. Thanks for meeting me.

SARAH
I almost didn't come.

JAMES
I'm glad you did.

He sits down across from her. An awkward silence.

SARAH
(pause, stirring her coffee)
It's been what, three years?

JAMES
Three years, two months, and fifteen days.

Sarah looks up, surprised he kept count.

SARAH
Why did you call?

JAMES
Because I made a mistake. A big one.

SARAH
(standing up)
That's not good enough anymore.

She grabs her coat. James reaches across the table.

JAMES
Sarah, wait. Please. Just hear me out.

Sarah pauses. She sits back down, arms crossed.

SARAH
(cold)
You have five minutes.

JAMES
When I left, I wasn't running away from you. I was running away from me. From who I had become. I was lost.

SARAH
So was I.

JAMES
I know. And I'm sorry. I've spent three years getting found. I want to find us again.

Sarah's expression softens slightly.

SARAH
(quietly)
I don't know if that's possible.

JAMES
Neither do I. But I had to try. I had to see if you'd even give me this chance.

Sarah looks out the window at the city passing by.

SARAH
I need time to think.

JAMES
I know. How much time?

SARAH
I don't know yet. But... call me next week.

She stands, finishes her coffee in one gulp, and walks toward the door.

JAMES
Sarah?

She turns back.

JAMES (CONT'D)
(voice breaking)
I really did miss you.

Sarah's face shows a flicker of the old connection, then she leaves.

James sits alone at the table, staring at her empty cup.

FADE OUT.

THE END
